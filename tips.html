Tips for a good night's sleep

Getting enough sleep is crucial for efficient functioning; it improves memory, increases attention, makes you feel energised and rested. However, sleep quality is as important as quantity! Here are a few tips that would make falling asleep and getting good quality sleep easier.

1. Get a proper amount of sleep.

As was discussed here, human sleep occurs in 90 minute cycles. Having five or six cycles per night is optimal and results in waking up rested and energised! Sleep calculator can help you in finding out the best times to fall asleep and wake up.

2. Ditch your smartphone!

Smartphones and computer screens emit a lot of blue light, simulating the sun's rays. It suppresses melatonin production in the brain making the body think it is still day. Staring at the screen before bed is the fastest way of becoming an insomniac! Thankfully, there are a couple of apps surpressing the blue light making the screen emit more yellow and red light. f.lux is an app that adapts the light emitted by the screen to the time of a day - a real lifesaver for anyone who stays up late looking at a screen!

3. Don't be afraid of naps.

Napping can be a good idea to boost productivity... or it can make you feel like you are hungover! Remeber the sleep cycles? It all connects to that! A 10 to 20 minute power-nap limits you to non-REM sleep and can increase alertness and energy levels. A 60 minute nap can make you remember facts, faces and names better. However, it includes slow-wave sleep, so it may increase the feeling of grogginess. A 90 minute nap is the whole sleep cycle, including non-REM and REM sleep. This length will make it easier to wake up feeling rested. Stay away from a 30 minute nap - it will make you feel more tires and groggy!

4. Follow bedtime rituals.

Our bodies like regularity. Going to bed at the same time every day will make it easier to wake up once the body is used to the routine. Taking a relaxing bath, reading a book or doing some light yoga exercises will help the body to wind down and put it into sleep mode. Listening to sleep music at the same time every day can be another way to calm down and fall asleep faster.

5. Say no to midnight pizza

Food and drink can affect the quality of sleep - everyone who has ever had a greasy meal just before bedtime can agree with that! Carbohydrates take a long time to digest which keeps the body awake. Drinking alcohol just before bed can also disturb sleep in healthy people. Caffeine will also make you awake - remember it is not only in coffee or energy drinks but also in tea, chocolate or soft drinks.
