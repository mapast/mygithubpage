the power of sleep

Dreaming

During REM phase of a sleep cycle vivid dreams can be formed. Dreams are a sequence of images, sounds, emotions and sensations happening while we are sleeping. They can be beautiful and joyful but also confusing and mysterious. Throughout history dreams have been associated with sacred revelation and prophecy. All this mystery can leave us wondering what dreams mean for the sleeper.

Dreams still remain a mystery even though modern science is so developed. The lenght of a dream can vary from a couple of seconds to 30 minutes[1]. On average, humans dream from three to seven times per night; however, they are quickly forgotten[2]. Dreams still remain a mystery even though modern science is so developed. Neither their function nor meaning has been discovered; in modern times, dreams has been conected to the unconscious mind.

Unconscious mind

In the late 19th century, psychotherapist Sigmund Freud developed a theory that the content of dreams is caused by the unconscious wish of fulfilment. He believed dreams reflected desires, often relating back to the dreamer's early childhood. Freud distinguished between the content of a dream (what the dreamer remembers) and symbolical meaning of the dream (like a desire or a wish). In his later work, Freud explored the possibility of universal symbols in dreams, especially sexual in nature[3]. He argued those symbols are personal rather than universal and dream dictionaries, still very popular now, were a soure of irritation to him.

Nightmares

Even though some exerts believe dreams are a random collection of images, many people report experiencing similar dreams: being chased, falling, being naked in front of people. These kind of dreams can be caused by anxieties but the source of stress has a different meaning for every person. Nightmares can be caused by stress, trauma, emotional problems or illness. They can also have physiological causes, like sleeping in an uncomfortable position, having a fever or eating just before bed[4] (Here you can find some tips on how to sleep better!). It can be a very distressing experience as people who suffer from chronic nightmares, just like insomniac, do not get enough sleep to feel rested and the quality of their life is affected.

Influence on art

Zdzislaw Beksinski was a Polish painter and sculptor known for his dystopian surrealistic paintings. He found inspiration for his art in dreams; the surreal art made by him is very grim, but also incredibly detailed. He often depicted anxiety in the form of torn or bandaged faces, skeletons, and deformed figures. Beksinski is a great example of how dreams can be surreal and horryfying and at the same time can be an inspiration for a great artist.
